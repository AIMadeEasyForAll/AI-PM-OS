# Skill: Roadmap

Use this skill to turn a prioritized backlog into a roadmap you can show to engineering, leadership, and customers without three different versions of the truth.

## When to use this

- Quarterly or half-year planning.
- A leadership review is coming up and you need a current, honest view of what's committed vs. exploratory.
- You're onboarding a new stakeholder who needs the "what's coming" picture fast.

## Inputs needed

- The output of [`prioritization.md`](prioritization.md) — a ranked, scoped list of candidate work.
- Known fixed dates (compliance deadlines, partner commitments, exec demos).
- Team capacity for the period (roughly, in person-weeks or sprints).

## Method

1. **Pick a roadmap format that matches the audience.** Now/Next/Later for leadership and customers; dated swimlanes for engineering; theme-based for cross-functional planning. Don't force one format on every audience.
2. **Group items into themes, not just a feature list.** A theme is the outcome being pursued (e.g. "reduce time-to-first-value"); features are how you pursue it. Themes survive scope changes; feature lists don't.
3. **Mark confidence per item.** Committed (date-bound, scoped, resourced), Likely (scoped, not fully resourced), Exploratory (theme only, no scope yet). Never present Exploratory as if it were Committed.
4. **State what's explicitly NOT on the roadmap.** A roadmap that only shows yeses can't be used to say no to a new request — that's half its job.
5. **Set a review cadence.** A roadmap not revisited monthly becomes fiction within a quarter. Say when it'll next be reviewed.

## Output

A roadmap doc with: themes, items per theme with confidence level, target timeframe (not exact dates unless truly committed), explicit out-of-scope list, and next review date.

## Common failure modes

- Dated roadmaps for Exploratory work — this is how "Q3" becomes a promise nobody remembers making.
- A roadmap that's really just the backlog, reformatted. If it doesn't say why these themes now, it's not a roadmap.
- Skipping the "not doing" section because it feels awkward to write. It's the most useful section for stakeholder conversations.
